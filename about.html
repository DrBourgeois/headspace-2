About

I am drawn to the liminal; the spaces, moments, and identities that exist between definitions. My work explores the fluid, the fragmented, and the cyclical, inviting a reconsideration of time, space, and existence. I am fascinated by the interplay of presence and absence, by rhythms that defy structure, and by the quiet tension between movement and stillness. I seek to create worlds where linearity unravels, where time becomes a tangled thread, a pause, or a possibility. Rooted in exploration and experimentation, my practice resists rigid frameworks, instead embracing multiplicity and openness.
